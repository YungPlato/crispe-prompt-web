A Beginner's Guide to the C.R.I.S.P.E. Prompting Framework

Published on September 3, 2025

If you want to level up your interactions with AI, moving beyond simple, one-line requests is key. Using a structured format, or a "prompting framework," provides the AI with the clarity and detail it needs to deliver truly exceptional results. One of the most comprehensive and effective frameworks available is C.R.I.S.P.E.

C.R.I.S.P.E. is an acronym that stands for Context, Role, Intent, Style, Persona, and Extras. By addressing each of these elements in your prompt, you leave very little room for ambiguity, guiding the AI toward the precise output you need. Let's break it down.

C: Context

Context sets the scene. It provides the background information the AI needs to understand the situation. What is the purpose of this task? What information is critical to its success?

Example: "I am launching a new e-commerce store that sells handmade leather goods."

R: Role

Assigning a role to the AI tells it what kind of expert it should be. This focuses its knowledge and skills on the task at hand. Instead of a generalist AI, you get a specialist.

Example: "You are an expert copywriter specializing in luxury e-commerce brands."

I: Intent

This is your core instruction. What, specifically, do you want the AI to do? Your intent should be clear, concise, and actionable.

Example: "Write three product descriptions for a new line of leather wallets."

S: Style

Style defines the "how" of the response. How should the AI write? What should the tone and voice be? This ensures the output matches your brand's personality.

Example: "The writing style should be sophisticated, elegant, and focus on craftsmanship and durability."

P: Persona

Persona is similar to Role but focuses more on the personality or character you want the AI to adopt. It adds a layer of flavor to the response.

Example: "Adopt the persona of a master artisan who has been working with leather for 50 years."

E: Extras

Extras are for any additional constraints or requirements. This could include formatting, word count, keywords to include, or things to avoid.

Example: "Each description must be between 100-150 words. Include the keywords 'full-grain leather' and 'hand-stitched'. Do not use overly casual language."

Putting It All Together

When you combine these elements, you get a rich, detailed prompt that gives the AI everything it needs. Here's how our example looks when fully assembled in the C.R.I.S.P.E. format:

C (Context): I am launching a new e-commerce store that sells handmade leather goods.

R (Role): You are an expert copywriter specializing in luxury e-commerce brands.

I (Intent): Write three product descriptions for a new line of leather wallets.

S (Style): The writing style should be sophisticated, elegant, and focus on craftsmanship and durability.

P (Persona): Adopt the persona of a master artisan who has been working with leather for 50 years.

E (Extras): Each description must be between 100-150 words. Include the keywords 'full-grain leather' and 'hand-stitched'. Do not use overly casual language.
